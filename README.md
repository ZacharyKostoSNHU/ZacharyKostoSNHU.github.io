# Zachary Kosto Computer Science ePortfolio


## Professional Self-Assessment

This portfolio highlights my growth, technical skills, and professional experience developed throughout the Computer Science program.

### Overview
Throughout my time in the Computer Science program at Southern New Hampshire University, I have developed both technical and professional skills that have prepared me to enter the field with confidence. Working through my coursework and completing my capstone project allowed me to not only strengthen my understanding of software development concepts, but also apply them in a way that reflects real-world expectations. By building and enhancing my Event Tracker application across software design, algorithms, and databases, I was able to take one project and continuously improve it, which helped me better understand how systems evolve and how different areas of computer science connect together. This process has helped shape my professional goals and reinforced my interest in working in either cybersecurity or data-focused roles as I continue building experience.

### Collaboration and Communication
One of the most valuable skills I developed throughout the program is collaboration. During my internships at Verizon Communications Inc., I worked with multiple security teams and regularly communicated research findings and technical insights. I presented a Flask-based dashboard I developed to stakeholders, explaining how it could improve detection and analysis workflows. These experiences helped me understand how collaboration goes beyond just working with other developers, and instead involves communicating clearly with different teams, including those with non-technical backgrounds. In both of my internships, I also presented my work to high-level stakeholders, which strengthened my ability to explain complex ideas in a way that is easy to understand. This directly aligns with the program outcome of designing and delivering professional-quality communication, as I learned how to adapt my communication style based on the audience.

### Technical Skills and Problem Solving
My experience in the program also helped me develop strong problem-solving skills through the use of algorithms and data structures. In my capstone project, I improved the efficiency of my application by introducing a HashSet to prevent duplicate events and using sorting to organize data more effectively. This allowed me to apply concepts like time complexity and data structure selection in a practical way. I learned how small changes in how data is handled can have a large impact on performance and usability. This aligns with the program outcome of designing and evaluating computing solutions, as I was able to analyze a problem, apply an appropriate solution, and improve the overall functionality of my application.

### Software Design and Database Development
In terms of software design and database development, I focused on creating a more structured and scalable application. I refactored my project to use a layered architecture, separating the user interface, business logic, and data access into different components. This made the application easier to maintain and more aligned with industry practices. I also redesigned the database to associate events with individual users using a user_id, which improved both data organization and privacy. This demonstrated my ability to use tools and techniques to build solutions that provide real value, which aligns with another core program outcome. These changes also reflect the type of work I completed during my internships, where I worked on improving systems and making them more efficient and reliable.

### Security Mindset
Developing a security mindset has been a key part of both my coursework and my professional experience. One important thing I learned is that attackers are constantly evolving, especially with the rise of AI, which is making threats more advanced and harder to detect. This has made me more aware of the importance of thinking from an attacker’s perspective and designing systems that reduce vulnerabilities. My experience as a member of InfraGard has also reinforced the importance of information sharing and protecting critical infrastructure. Throughout my work, I have focused on writing code that considers security, avoids exposing sensitive data, and supports safer system design.

### Portfolio Integration
The artifacts included in my ePortfolio reflect my growth across all areas of the Computer Science program. My Event Tracker application serves as the central artifact, with each enhancement demonstrating a different area of expertise. The software design enhancement shows my ability to improve application structure, the algorithms enhancement highlights my understanding of efficiency and data handling, and the database enhancement demonstrates my ability to design and implement user-focused data storage solutions. Together, these artifacts provide a complete view of my technical skills and how I apply them to solve problems.

### Conclusion and Career Goals
Overall, this program has helped me build a strong foundation in computer science while also preparing me for my professional career. What sets me apart is my combination of academic experience and real-world exposure through my internships, along with my focus on both cybersecurity and data. In the future, I plan to use my technical skills to support law enforcement and contribute to security-related efforts, while continuing to gain experience in data and cybersecurity roles. This ePortfolio represents not only what I have learned, but also what I am capable of doing as I move forward in my career.
